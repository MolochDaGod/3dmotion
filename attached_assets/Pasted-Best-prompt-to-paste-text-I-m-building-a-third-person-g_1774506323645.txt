text
I’m building a third-person game with Three.js + Rapier. My character can move faster than the camera catches up, and I get rubber-banding / jitter / laggy follow behavior.

I want you to act like a senior gameplay/engine programmer and audit my third-person controller + follow camera setup.

Your job:
1. Find the exact cause of the rubber-banding, jitter, or camera lag.
2. Check whether the issue comes from:
   - physics timestep vs render timestep mismatch
   - camera updating before physics/body sync
   - mesh position and rigidbody position being updated separately
   - direct position/translation setting on a dynamic rigidbody
   - bad lerp/damping math
   - stale transforms / wrong delta usage
   - camera-relative movement math being incorrect
   - parent/child transform issues
   - rotation yaw/pitch math errors
   - wrong use of setTranslation, setLinvel, impulses, or kinematic APIs
3. Rewrite the code using best practices for a modern third-person action camera.
4. Keep movement responsive, camera smooth, and prevent visible lag between player and camera.
5. Explain what was wrong and why.

Technical requirements:
- Three.js for rendering
- Rapier for physics
- Third-person action game
- Camera should smoothly follow behind player
- Player movement should be camera-relative
- Movement should feel tight, not floaty
- Avoid frame-rate-dependent behavior
- Use proper delta-based smoothing
- Use rigidbody as the source of truth for position
- Do not move mesh and rigidbody separately
- If dynamic body is wrong for this setup, say whether kinematic is better and why

What I need back:
1. A diagnosis of the problem
2. A corrected version of my movement + camera code
3. Notes on best practices for third-person follow cameras in Three.js + Rapier
4. Any math issues in my direction, offset, interpolation, or lookAt logic
5. Suggested values for camera distance, height, damping, and player speed

Important:
- Be specific.
- Do not give generic advice.
- Point to the exact lines/patterns causing desync.
- If multiple systems are fighting each other, identify each one.
- Prefer a practical production-ready fix.

Here is my code:
[PASTE movement code]
[PASTE rigidbody setup]
[PASTE camera follow code]
[PASTE useFrame / update loop / physics step]
